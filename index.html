Every Nigerian citizen has rights as provided under the Constitution of the federal republic of Nigeria, 1999 (As Amended). We will briefly examine what is fundamental human right before going ahead to list the rights as contained in the Constitution.

What is fundamental human right

Fundamental human rights simply put, are those rights accruing to an individual because he is human. These rights are inalienable. They are contained in Chapter IV of the Nigerian Constitution,1999 (as amended).

Rights provided for in the Nigerian Constitution, 1999 (as amended)

  1. Right to Life
  2. Right to dignity of human person
  3. Right to personal liberty
  4. Right to fair hearing
  5. Right to private and family life
  6. Right to freedom of thought, conscience and religion
  7. Right to freedom of expression and the press
  8. Right to freedom of assembly
  9. Right to freedom of movement
  10. Right to freedom from discrimination
